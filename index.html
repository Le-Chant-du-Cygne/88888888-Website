88888888

88888888 is a game played with a specific apparatus that allows players to manipulate color filters to make the game space visible and understandable.



Concept

88888888 is a first person exploration game in which players use a specific device that allows them to switch the color filter through which they see the game space. Each filter reveals different parts of the world. Players must find their way through the maze by changing filters and revealing places, doors, stairways and hallways that she could not see otherwise.

The Helmet

88888888 uses a custom mechanical helmet featuring three different positions associated with three different color filters: red, blue and green. Players can switch from one filter to another by using the handles on each of the helmet's sides.

Creation of the helmet

The helmet was built by laser cutting 3mm thick plexiglass plates and then assembling the various parts together. The last layer of polarizing filter was removed from the LCD screen, becoming all white and unusable. We then added a polarizing filter onto the color filters of the helmet so that players cannot cheat by taking the helmet off.

Story

88888888 is a game created by Le Chant du Cygne collective (Simon Chauvin, Romain Enselme and Gaël Bourhis).

The development of the project started during the game jam Mozilla Lumière in Paris, and continued after that during our stay at the Gamelier in the Center for Research and Interdisciplinarity in Paris. We also had access to the CRI's fab lab, allowing us to build a custom helmet for the game.

88888888 was exhibited during a party organized by Take The Blows at the Badaboom venue.
